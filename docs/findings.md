## Key Findings:

1. RRB Infrastructure Crisis

Uttar Pradesh Gramin Bank, worst performer with 5.58% weighted 
TD% as remitter and only 88% approval rate.
Andhra Pradesh Grameena Bank, 3.58% TD% remitter, 84% approval.
Most of the worst performers are Regional Rural Banks (RRBs), many of which were affected by the May 2025 One State One RRB consolidation program.

2. Remitter vs Beneficiary Gap

Same banks perform significantly worse as remitters than 
beneficiaries. UP Gramin Bank: 5.58% TD remitter vs 3.95% 
beneficiary. Suggests outgoing transaction processing is 
weaker than incoming.

3. India Post Volume Risk  

India Post Payments Bank, 1.21% TD% but handles 13,158 Mn 
transactions. Highest absolute failure volume in the dataset.

4. UP Gramin Bank Recovery Then Regression

TD% peaked at 36.31% in Jan 2024, over 1 in 3 transactions 
failing. Improved dramatically to 0.28% by Sep 2024. Then 
spiked again to 10.22% in Sep 2025, likely due to One State 
One RRB merger integration disruption in May 2025.

5. Remitter Data Drives Revenue Impact Analysis

Revenue impact calculations use remitter data only. The remitter 
side represents the bank processing the outgoing payment,here 
bank infrastructure failures matter most to the customer 
experience. Beneficiary failures are less visible to the sender.

6. Banks Bad at Both Roles

UP Gramin Bank, Andhra Pradesh Grameena Bank, Karnataka Grameena 
Bank, Maharashtra Gramin Bank, and Rajasthan Gramin Bank are all 
bad at both remitter and beneficiary roles. These 5 banks touch 
every transaction they're involved in with elevated failure rates.

7. Ecosystem Success Rate Declining

UPI ecosystem approved% dropped from 94.38% in Jan 2024 to 
90.54% in Feb 2026, nearly 4 percentage points in 26 months.
Driver is BD% doubling from 4.86% to 9.26%, not TD%. Suggests 
user-side failures increasing as UPI expands to new user segments,
not bank infrastructure degrading.

8. Scale Brings Reliability

Perfect inverse relationship between transaction volume and TD%.
Very high volume banks average 0.38% TD vs 1.90% for low volume 
banks. Larger banks invest more in infrastructure reliability.

9. NSDL Payments Bank Asymmetry

2.92% TD as remitter but only 0.78% as beneficiary. Specifically 
bad at sending money. Suggests outgoing transaction processing 
infrastructure has a specific weakness.

10. Revenue Impact Rankings

SBI tops absolute transaction value at risk (Rs 97,630 Cr) due 
to volume, not poor performance (0.52% TD%).
UP Gramin Bank causes Rs 8,572 Cr at risk with only 974 Mn 
volume, disproportionate damage from 5.58% TD%.
HDFC Bank benchmark: 36,122 Mn volume but only Rs 4,053 Cr at 
risk due to 0.07% TD%. Industry gold standard.
Key reframe: This metric represents transaction value at risk, 
not revenue lost. Actual loss depends on retry rates, 
customer friction, and merchant impact.

11. The "What If HDFC Standard" Scenario
If every bank matched HDFC's 0.07% TD%, total transaction value 
at risk would drop by approximately 85%. The gap between worst 
and best performers represents the addressable improvement 
opportunity.
